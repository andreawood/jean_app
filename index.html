Let's Talk Denim

Hey there! This app is meant to help women learn about and style denim jeans. Denim jeans are a durable and versatile artcile of clothing that can be a staple piece in any wardrobe and styled in many different ways!

Styles

What's Your Rise?

Click on the images to learn more about rise.

Low Rise

Sits two inches below the belly button

Ideal for those with curvier hips and athletic body types. Ensure they fit snug and have stretch so it can accentuate the hips!

Mid Rise

Sits just below the belly button

This is considered the most figure-friendly fit! It is comfortable for all body types and structures the waist line beautifully.

High Rise

Sits at or above the belly button

The most classic fit of all! Looks great on those with smaller waists and slender bodies. Pairs well with cropped and tucked tops.

What's Your Fit?

Click on the images to learn more about fit.

Skinny

Suits all body types

This fit is the slimmest at the ankle. It pairs well with fitted, loose, and even oversized tops!

Straight Leg

Apple & Athletic body types

Wider at the ankle. Still achieves a slimming effect without a skin-tight fit of skinny jeans.

Flare

Pear & Hourglass body types

Gets its name for it's 10 inch, bell-like bottom hem. Pro tip: fit runs long so choose heel height accordingly.

Bootcut

Pear & Hourglass body types

A wide-leg style that doesn't go out of style! Wear this fit with 1.5 to 3-inch heeled booties.

Boyfriend

Apple & Athletic body types

A loose/comfortable fit perfect for a laid back look! This fit typically pairs well with fitted tops.

How to Measure

Hover over each div for measurement instructions and visual guide.

Waist

Meaure the smallest part of your waist as tight as you'd like to wear the waistband of the jeans.

Abdomen

Measure 3 to 4 inches below waistline around the fullest part of your abdomen.

Hips

Measure the fullest part of your hips.

Thigh

Measure just below the crotch, all the way around the fullest part of your upper leg.

Inseam

Measure from the croth to the ankle at the inner seam to determine leg length.

Crotch Length

While standing, measure from the front of the waistline to the center of the body and then from the center of the body to the back of the waistline.

Crotch Depth

Sit on a flat chair and measure the contour of the body from waistline to hip. Hold the tape straight from hip to the bottom of the chair.

History of Denim Jeans

Have you ever heard of Levi's jeans? It's short for the brand Levis Strauss & Co. which was founded by German business man Levi Strauss. You can learn more about Levi Strauss here. Click the button below to expand a timeline that describes Levi's rise to fame.

1829: Levi Strauss

Levi Strauss is born in Buttenheim, Bavaria which is a landlocked state in Germany.

Image source: Unknown author, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

1853: Levi Strauss & Co.

Strauss arrives to San Francisco and opens a wholesale dry goods business. He immediately starts donating money to orphanages and natural disaster relief funds which starts the companys tradition of sharing prosperity with the community.

Image Source: Cullen328 photo by Jim Heaphy, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

1873: Blue Denim Jeans

Levi Strauss & Co. and Jacob Davis, a Nevada tailor, become partners and get a patent pon what is considered an "Improvement in Fastening Pocket-Openings". The process of adding metal rivets to work pants would create stronger, more durable pants for working men. These would later become known as blue jeans.

Image Source: Marcos André, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

1886: Two Horse Trademark

In the face of competition, the Two Horse Trademark depicts two horses trying to pull apart a pair of Levi's blue denim jeans. This became a symbol for the garment's strength and durability.

Image Source: Ludovic Glucksman at French Wikipedia., CC BY 1.0, via Wikimedia Commons

1934: Levi's for Women

After years in the market, Levi Strauss & Co. finally introduced the first blue denim jeans for women.

Image Source. Pexels.com

1965: Going Global

The 1960's marked a surge in popularity for jeans, jackets and other Levi's products which allowed the company to open global offices in Europe and Asia.

Image source: grahamc99, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

1986: Levi's Dockers

The Dockers khakis launch revolutionizes business attire, making casual wear appropriate attire for the workplace.

Image source: ‏🌸🙌 في عین الله on Unsplash

2015: Green Practices

Levi Strauss & Co. launches a Screened Chemistry Program which researches health and environmental impacts of chemicals to find safter alternatives for product innovation.
